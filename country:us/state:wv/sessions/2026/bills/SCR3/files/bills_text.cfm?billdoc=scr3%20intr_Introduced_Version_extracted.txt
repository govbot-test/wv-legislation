Red = new code to be enacted SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 3(By Senator Oliverio)[Introduced February 13, 2026] Requesting the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study how to fund the Flood Resiliency Fund.Whereas, Flooding is one of the most frequent and destructive natural disasters in West Virginia, causing loss of life, property damage, disruption of emergency services, and long-term economic impacts; and Whereas, In 2017, the West Virginia Legislature recognized the importance of flood resiliency through the creation of the State Resiliency Office within the Department of Commerce and created the Joint Legislative Committee on Flooding through the passage of House Bill 2935 to coordinate disaster resilient strategies and study flood protection efforts across the state; and Whereas, In 2023, the Legislature passed Senate Bill 677, creating the West Virginia Flood Resiliency Trust Fund to be administered by the State Resiliency Officer, authorizing an initial allocation of up to $40,000,000 from the General Revenue Fund and annual replenishment requests of up to $40,000,000; and Whereas, The State Resiliency Office published the West Virginia Flood Resiliency Plan in 2024 as required by Senate Bill 677, establishing a comprehensive framework; andWhereas, While the Flood Resiliency Plan establishes clear priorities and goals, the State currently lacks a comprehensive, coordinated, and sustainable funding strategy to capitalize and sustain the Flood Resiliency Trust Fund at a level sufficient to support implementation of those priorities; andWhereas, The Flood Resiliency Plan identifies six core goals: (1) improving flood data and mapping; (2) updating flood and community planning; (3) implementing stream and watershed interventions; (4) protecting critical infrastructure; (5) ensuring emergency access and response capabilities; and (6) advancing long-term risk reduction measures; and Whereas, During the 2025 regular session, there were multiple efforts to increase investment in the Flood Resiliency Trust Fund signaling growing legislative interest in identifying viable funding sources; andWhereas, A thorough examination of potential revenue sources and federal matching opportunities is necessary to identify the most effective and sustainable means of capitalizing the Flood Resiliency Trust Fund; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Legislature hereby requests the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study how to fund the Flood Resiliency Fund; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study shall include, but not be limited to, an examination of the following:(1) Dedicated state revenue streams, including but not limited to allocations from the severance tax, insurance surcharges, or other existing state revenue sources that may be directed to the Flood Resiliency Trust Fund on a recurring basis;(2) Federal matching funds and grant programs, including the Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities program, Community Development Block Grant disaster recovery funds, and other federal programs that may be leveraged to maximize the impact of state investments in flood resilience;(3) The experience and best practices of other states that have established dedicated flood resilience or natural disaster mitigation funds, including their funding mechanisms, governance structures, and outcomes;(4) The appropriate initial capitalization level and ongoing annual funding amount necessary to meaningfully advance the goals of the West Virginia Flood Resiliency Plan; and(5) The feasibility and fiscal impact of directing an allocation from excess monies held in the Income Tax Reserve Fund, established pursuant to section 11-21-96 of the Code of West Virginia, to provide initial or ongoing capitalization of the Flood Resiliency Trust Fund; and, be itFurther resolved, That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance is requested to report its findings, conclusions, and recommendations, including proposed legislation, to the Senate no later than January 1, 2027, so that any resulting legislation may be considered during the 2027 session.

§15-2-5. Career progression system state; salaries; exclusion from wage and hour laws, with supplemental payment; bond; leave time for members called to duty in guard or reserves.

Each member of the West Virginia State Police whose salary is fixed and specified in this annual salary schedule is entitled to the length of service increases set forth in subsection (e) of this section and supplemental pay as provided in subsection (g) of this section.

(e) Each member of the West Virginia State Police whose salary is fixed and specified pursuant to this section shall receive, and is entitled to, an increase in salary over that set forth in subsection (d) of this section for grade in rank, based on length of service, including that service served before and after the effective date of this section with the West Virginia State Police as follows: Beginning on January 1, 2015, and continuing thereafter, at the end of two years of service with the West Virginia State Police, the member shall receive a salary increase of $500 to be effective during his or her next year of service and a like increase at yearly intervals thereafter, with the increases to be cumulative.  The forensic laboratory employees whose salaries are fixed and specified pursuant to this section, shall receive, and are entitled to, an increase in salary over that set forth in subsection (d) of this section, in accordance with §15-2-7(h) of this code.

(f) In applying the salary schedules set forth in this section where salary increases are provided for length of service, members of the West Virginia State Police in service at the time the schedules become effective shall be given credit for prior service and shall be paid the salaries the same length of service entitles them to receive under the provisions of this section.

(g) The Legislature finds and declares that because of the unique duties of members of the West Virginia State Police, it is not appropriate to apply the provisions of state wage and hour laws to them. Accordingly, members of the West Virginia State Police are excluded from the provisions of state wage and hour laws. This express exclusion shall not be construed as any indication that the members were or were not covered by the wage and hour laws prior to this exclusion.

In lieu of any overtime pay they might otherwise have received under the wage and hour laws, and in addition to their salaries and increases for length of service, members who have completed basic training and who are exempt from federal Fair Labor Standards Act guidelines may receive supplemental pay as provided in this section.

The authority of the superintendent to propose a legislative rule or amendment thereto for promulgation in accordance with §29A-3-1 et seq. of this code to establish the number of hours per month which constitute the standard pay period for the members of the West Virginia State Police is hereby continued. The rule shall further establish, on a graduated hourly basis, the criteria for receipt of a portion or all of supplemental payment when hours are worked in excess of the standard pay period. The superintendent shall certify at least twice per month to the West Virginia State Police payroll officer the names of those members who have worked in excess of the standard pay period and the amount of their entitlement to supplemental payment. The supplemental payment may not exceed $200 per pay period. The superintendent and civilian employees of the West Virginia State Police are not eligible for any supplemental payments.

(h) Each member of the West Virginia State Police, except the superintendent and civilian employees, shall execute, before entering upon the discharge of his or her duties, a bond with security in the sum of $5,000 payable to the State of West Virginia, conditioned upon the faithful performance of his or her duties, and the bond shall be approved as to form by the Attorney General and as to sufficiency by the Governor.

(i) In consideration for compensation paid by the West Virginia State Police to its members during those members’ participation in the West Virginia State Police Cadet Training Program pursuant to §30-29-8 of this code, the West Virginia State Police may require of its members by written agreement entered into with each of them in advance of such participation in the program that, if a member should voluntarily discontinue employment any time within one year immediately following completion of the training program, he or she shall be obligated to pay to the West Virginia State Police a pro rata portion of such compensation equal to that part of such year which the member has chosen not to remain in the employ of the West Virginia State Police.

(j) Any member of the West Virginia State Police who is called to perform active duty training or inactive duty training in the National Guard or any reserve component of the armed forces of the United States annually shall be granted, upon request, leave time not to exceed 30 calendar days for the purpose of performing the active duty training or inactive duty training and the time granted may not be deducted from any leave accumulated as a member of the West Virginia State Police.

ARTICLE 2. WEST VIRGINIA STATE POLICE.

§15-2-5a. State minimum salary supplement for market pay.  

(a) Definitions. — For the purposes of this section, the terms defined in this section have the meanings ascribed to them unless a different meaning is clearly required by the context in which the term is used:

"Border state" means any of the five states that border the state of West Virginia.

"Contiguous county" means any county, regardless of which state it is situated in, that borders on the county of employment of a member of the state police.  

“County specific median income” means the median income of the county of employment of a member of the state police, as determined by the most recently available United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report.  

"Market pay enhancement" means a positive result obtained when subtracting the West Virginia median income from the regional median income and dividing the difference by the West Virginia median income; subtracting the West Virginia median income from the county specific median income and dividing the difference by the West Virginia median income; and then multiplying the highest of the two preceding figures by the statewide average state-funded salary of a member of the state police. The market pay enhancement may not exceed 20 percent of the statewide average state-funded salary of a member of the state police’s base pay in this state.

"Region" means the county in which a member of the state police is employed and each contiguous county to that county.  A contiguous county may be a county in a border state.

"Regional income" means the average of the median income of the county of employment and the counties contiguous to that county of employment of the state police as determined by the most recently available United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report to be used for the calculation as required by this section.

“State Police” shall mean all members of the West Virginia State Police, excluding the Superintendent, as set forth in §15-2-1 et seq. of this code.

“Superintendent” means the Superintendent of the West Virginia State Police as set forth in §15-2-1 et seq. of this code.

"West Virginia median income" means that figure as determined by the most recently available United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report to be used for the calculation as required in this section.

(b) (1) Market pay for state police multiplier. — Beginning on December 1, 2026, and continuing on the first day of December every year thereafter, the superintendent shall determine:

(A) The most recent available West Virginia median income based on data available from the United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report;

(B) The most recent available regional median income for the immediately preceding calendar year based on data available from the United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report; and

(C) The most recent available county specific median income for the immediately preceding calendar year based on data available from the United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report. A county specific median income shall be calculated for each of West Virginia’s 55 counties.  

(2) These figures shall be used to determine the multiplier used to set the amount of a market pay enhancement.  In order to determine the multiplier to set the amount of the market pay enhancement, the superintendent shall determine two multipliers and the higher of the two multipliers shall be used to calculate the market pay enhancement. The superintendent shall calculate the first multiplier by deducting the West Virginia median income from the regional median income.  This figure, divided by the amount of the West Virginia median income, is the first multiplier.  The superintendent shall calculate the second multiplier by deducting the West Virginia median income from the county specific median income, and this figure divided by the amount of the West Virginia median income is the second multiplier. The higher of these two multipliers shall be used in calculating the market pay enhancement.  The multiplier shall be rounded to the nearest whole percentage.  

(c) Calculation of market pay enhancement.  — The higher of the multipliers as determined by subsection (b) of this section shall be multiplied by the statewide average state-funded state police base salary in this state.  State Police in that county shall receive that amount as a market pay enhancement annually, effective starting July 1, 2027: Provided, That this amount shall not exceed 20 percent of the statewide average state-funded salary of a member of the state police base salary in this state.  

(d) Distribution. — State funds for this purpose shall be allocated to the West Virginia State Police in the annual Executive Budget. Funding shall be allocated for this purpose for all members of the state police, excluding the superintendent, regardless of the funding source utilized to compensate the members of the state police.

(e) Applicability of payments. — These market pay enhancement payments shall be in addition to any amounts prescribed in the applicable Annual Salary Schedule (Base Pay) set forth in §15-2-5 of this code.

(f) Grievance or cause of action. -- No right to grievance or private cause of action, either express or implied, is created, may be inferred, or otherwise may arise from the provisions or enactment of the policy.

(g) Reporting. — The superintendent shall prepare a report to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance in any year where a market pay enhancement is required pursuant to the provisions of this section, detailing any relevant information regarding market pay enhancements for members of the state police.  

(h) Additional enhancements. — Nothing in this section prevents the superintendent from providing, in a uniform manner, market pay enhancements greater than those required by this section.

(i) Rulemaking. — Notwithstanding any provision of this code to the contrary, the superintendent may propose rules for legislative approval in accordance with the provisions of §29A-3-1 et seq., explaining and implementing this section.

ARTICLE 4. SALARIES, WAGES AND OTHER BENEFITS.

 

§18A-4-2d. State minimum salary supplement for professional persons and service personnel for market pay.  

(a) Definitions. — For the purposes of this section, the terms defined in this section have the meanings ascribed to them unless a different meaning is clearly required by the context in which the term is used:

"Board" shall mean the State Board of Education.

"Border state" means any of the five states that border the state of West Virginia.

"Contiguous county" means any county, regardless of which state it is situated in, that borders on the county of employment of a professional person or school service personnel.  

“County specific median income” means the median income of the county of employment of a professional person or school service personnel, as determined by the most recently available United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report.  

"Market pay enhancement" means a positive result obtained when subtracting the West Virginia median income from the regional median income and dividing the difference by the West Virginia median income; subtracting the West Virginia median income from the county specific median income and dividing the difference by the West Virginia median income; and then multiplying the highest of the two preceding figures by the statewide average state-funded basic salary of professional persons in this state or the average state-funded basic salary of school service personnel in this state, whichever is appropriate. The market pay enhancement may not exceed 20 percent of the statewide average state-funded basic salary of professional persons in this state or the average state-funded basic salary of school service personnel in this state, whichever is appropriate.

“Professional person” or “professional persons” means the same as that term is defined in §18A-1-1 of this code.  A “professional person” shall include:

  1. “Professional educator” as that term is defined in §18A-1-1 of this code;
  2.  “Other professional employee” as that term is defined in §18A-1-1 of this code; and
  3. “Long-term substitute” as that term is defined in §18A-1-1 of this code.

"Region" means the county in which a professional person or service personnel is employed and each contiguous county to that county.  A contiguous county may be a county in a border state.

"Regional median income" means the average of the median income of the county of employment and the counties contiguous to that county of employment of a professional person or service personnel, as determined by the most recently available United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report to be used for the calculation as required by this section.

“Service personnel shall mean the same as that term is defined in §18A-1-1 of this code.   

"West Virginia median income" means that figure as determined by the most recently available United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report to be used for the calculation as required in this section.

(b) (1) Market pay for professional persons or school service personnel multiplier. — Beginning on December 1, 2026, and continuing on the first day of December every year thereafter, the state board shall determine:

(A) The most recent available West Virginia median income based on data available from the United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report;

(B) The most recently available regional median income based on data available from the United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report; and

(C) The most recently available county specific median income based on data available from the United States Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Report. A county specific median income shall be calculated for each of West Virginia’s 55 counties.  

(2) These figures shall be used to determine the multiplier used to set the amount of a market pay enhancement.  In order to determine the multiplier to set the amount of the market pay enhancement, the state board shall determine two multipliers and the higher of the two multipliers shall be used to calculate the market pay enhancement. The state board shall calculate the first multiplier by deducting the West Virginia income from the regional median income.  This figure, divided by the amount of the West Virginia median income, is the first multiplier.  The state board shall calculate the second multiplier by deducting the West Virginia median income from the county specific median income, and this figure divided by the amount of the West Virginia median income is the second multiplier. The higher of these two multipliers shall be used in calculating the market pay enhancement.  The multiplier shall be rounded to the nearest whole percentage.  

(c) Calculation of market pay enhancement.  — The higher of the multipliers as determined by subsection (b) of this section shall be multiplied by the statewide average state-funded professional person’s basic salary or the statewide average state-funded school personnel basic salary, whichever is appropriate.  A professional person or school service personnel in that county shall receive that amount as a market pay enhancement annually, effective starting July 1, 2027: Provided, That this amount shall not exceed 20 percent of the statewide average state-funded basic salary of professional persons in this state or the statewide average state-funded basic salary of school service personnel in this state, whichever is appropriate.

(d) Distribution. — State funds for this purpose shall be paid within the West Virginia public school support plan in accordance with §18-9A-4 of this code. Funding shall be allocated for this purpose for all professional persons or school service personnel, regardless of the funding source utilized to compensate the professional person’s basic salary or the school service personnel basic salary whichever is appropriate.

(e) Applicability of payments. — These market pay enhancement payments shall be in addition to any amounts prescribed in the applicable State Minimum Salary Schedule set forth in §18A-4-2 of this code, or the Service Personnel Minimum Monthly Salary Schedule set forth in §18A-4-8a of this code, any specific additional amounts prescribed in this section and article and any county supplement in effect in a county pursuant to §18A-4-5a or §18A-4-5b of this code.

(f) Grievance or cause of action. -- No right to grievance or private cause of action, either express or implied, is created, may be inferred, or otherwise may arise from the provisions or enactment of the policy.

(g) Reporting. — The state board shall prepare a report to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance in any year where a market pay enhancement is required pursuant to the provisions of this section, detailing any relevant information regarding market pay enhancements for professional persons and school service personnel.  

(h) Additional enhancements. — Nothing in this section prevents a county board from providing, in a uniform manner, market pay enhancements greater than those required by this section.

(i) Rulemaking. — Notwithstanding any provision of this code to the contrary, the state board may propose rules for legislative approval in accordance with the provisions of §29A-3-1 et seq., explaining and implementing this section.

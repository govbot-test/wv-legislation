Red = new code to be enacted SENATE RESOLUTION 15(By Senators Martin, Queen, Hamilton, Phillips, Rose, Smith (Mr. President), and Barnhart)[Introduced January 27, 2026] Recognizing the 154th anniversary of Glenville State University.Whereas, In February 1872, the passage of West Virginia Senate Bill 52 allowed for a branch of the state normal school to be established at Glenville; andWhereas, The Glenville Branch of the West Virginia State Normal School was originally founded in order to serve the educational needs of West Virginia citizens by preparing teachers to serve in classrooms across the state; andWhereas, Glenville State University has expanded its mission to now prepare students for careers not only in teaching, but in business, land resources, criminal justice, music and art, health sciences, language arts, and human services; andWhereas, Glenville State University continues to be critical to the educational and economic vitality of central West Virginia and beyond through a variety of educational and professional degrees at the associate, baccalaureate, and master’s levels; andWhereas, Glenville State University continues to be a vital educational resource to the Mountain State and beyond; andWhereas, Throughout its long history, the faculty and staff of Glenville State University have served its students with distinction through an individualized, career-focused approach; andWhereas, Glenville State University students, employees, and alumni, actively support their communities financially and otherwise; therefore, be itResolved by the Senate:That the Senate hereby recognizes the 154th anniversary of Glenville State University; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Senate extends its sincere appreciation and gratitude to Glenville State University for its contributions to the State of West Virginia; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Clerk is hereby directed to forward a copy of this resolution to the president of Glenville State University, Dr. Mark A. Manchin.

Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE concurrent RESOLUTION 26(By Delegates G. Howell, Mallow, Toney, Young, Holstein, D. Cannon, Hanshaw (Mr. Speaker), and Green[Introduced March 3, 2026; referred to the Committee on Rules] Requesting the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study the feasibility of implementing energy recovery and micro-hydropower technologies within public water systems in West Virginia to reduce operating costs and mitigate future water rate increases.Whereas, West Virginia has some of the highest residential water rates in the nation, placing a financial burden on households and creating barriers to economic development, housing growth, and industrial recruitment; andWhereas, Public water systems in West Virginia face high operating costs due to aging infrastructure, energy intensive treatment processes, mountainous terrain requiring pumping, and small customer bases that must absorb fixed costs; andWhereas, Energy costs represent a significant portion of water system operating expenses, particularly in systems that rely on pumping, pressure management, and treatment processes; andWhereas, Many public water systems contain existing infrastructure, including pressure reducing valves, transmission lines, plant discharge points, and low head dams, where excess hydraulic energy is currently dissipated and could be recovered through micro-hydropower or in-pipe turbine technologies; andWhereas, Energy recovery technologies installed within water systems can generate electricity to offset plant power consumption, improve operational efficiency, and reduce long term operating costs without requiring major new infrastructure; andWhereas, Reducing operating costs through energy recovery can help stabilize water rates by lowering the financial burden on ratepayers and improving system sustainability; therefore, be itResolved by the House of Delegates:That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance is hereby requested to study the feasibility of implementing energy recovery and micro-hydropower technologies within public water systems in West Virginia; and, be it Further Resolved, That the study include, but not be limited to, the following:1. Identification of public water systems with suitable conditions for energy recovery, including pressure reducing valve sites, transmission line pressure drops, treatment plant discharge points, and low head dam facilities;2. Evaluation of in-pipe hydropower and other conduit based technologies that can generate electricity without significant environmental impact or major new construction;3. Analysis of potential cost savings from onsite power generation and the resulting impact on operating expenses and water rates;4. Review of federal and state funding opportunities, including USDA and Department of Energy programs, that could support installation of energy recovery systems;5. Examination of regulatory and permitting considerations associated with micro-hydropower installations in public water systems;6. Development of recommendations for pilot projects and statewide implementation strategies to improve water system efficiency and affordability; and, be it Further Resolved, That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance report its findings, conclusions, and recommendations to the Legislature no later than December 1, 2026.

Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 25(By Delegates G. Howell, Mallow, Toney, Young, Holstein, D. Cannon, Hanshaw (Mr. Speaker) and Green)[Introduced March 3, 2026; referred to the Committee on Rules ] Requesting the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study strategies to reduce non-revenue water in public water systems through leak detection, prioritization of high loss systems, reinvestment of cost savings into repairs, performance based incentives for efficient systems, and potential financing mechanisms, including special bond programs.Whereas, West Virginia consistently ranks among the highest in the nation for residential water rates, placing a financial burden on households and hindering economic development and housing growth; andWhereas, Many public water systems in West Virginia experience significant non-revenue water losses due to leaks, aging infrastructure, inaccurate metering, and system inefficiencies; andWhereas, Non-revenue water represents treated and pumped water that generates no revenue, increasing operating costs and shifting the financial burden onto paying customers; andWhereas, Reducing water loss can increase system revenue, lower operating costs, improve reliability, and stabilize long term water rates without increasing charges to customers; andWhereas, Targeting systems with the highest levels of water loss can maximize cost savings and improve statewide water system sustainability; andWhereas, Reinvesting cost savings from reduced water loss into infrastructure repairs can create a self sustaining cycle of system improvement; andWhereas, Performance based incentives can encourage efficient system management by rewarding water systems that achieve and maintain low levels of non-revenue water; andWhereas, Innovative financing mechanisms, including special bond programs, revolving funds, reduced interest loans, and grant prioritization for high performing systems, may provide cost effective pathways to accelerate system improvements; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Legislature hereby requests the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study strategies to reduce non-revenue water in public water systems across West Virginia; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study include, but not be limited to, an examination of the following:(1) Identification and prioritization of public water systems with the highest levels of non- revenue water;(2) Evaluation of leak detection technologies, asset management practices, metering improvements, and pressure management strategies that can reduce water loss;(3) Analysis of the financial impact of non-revenue water on system operating costs and customer rates;(4) Development of strategies to reinvest cost savings from reduced water loss into infrastructure repairs and system upgrades;(5) Examination of the feasibility of establishing state supported leak detection programs, shared equipment pools, or technical assistance teams for small systems;(6) Evaluation of financing mechanisms, including special bond programs, revolving funds, and performance based funding, to support leak reduction initiatives;(7) Examination of performance based incentives, including:(a) Priority access to expansion and infrastructure grants for systems demonstrating low non-revenue water;(b) Reduced interest rates, principal forgiveness, or favorable loan terms for high performing systems;(c) Expedited permitting or project review for systems meeting efficiency benchmarks; and(d) Grant programs tied to verified reductions in water loss;(8) Assessment of safeguards to ensure small and rural systems receive technical assistance and reasonable pathways to qualify for incentive programs; and(9) Assessment of potential statewide cost savings and rate stabilization resulting from reduced water loss; and, be it Further resolved, That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance is requested to report its findings, conclusions, and recommendations to the Legislature no later than December 1, 2026.

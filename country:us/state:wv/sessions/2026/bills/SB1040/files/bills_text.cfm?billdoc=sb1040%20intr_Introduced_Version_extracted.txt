Siting certificates for certain electric generating facilities or material modifications thereof. [Repealed.] §24-2-23. West Virginia Energy Freedom Act. (a) Short title. – This section is and may be cited as the "West Virginia Energy Freedom Act". (b) Legislative findings. – The Legislature finds that streamlining the approval process for new electric generation facilities, particularly dispatchable resources such as natural gas, and for coal-fired plants, is essential to promoting economic development, enhancing energy reliability, attracting investment in the state’s abundant natural resources, and supporting job creation throughout the state.(c) Purpose. – The purpose of this section is to reform the regulatory framework for new electric generation facilities by:(1) Eliminating the mandatory certificate of public convenience and necessity or siting certificate for a non-utility project;(2) Shifting oversight to a simplified registration process, local permitting, environmental compliance, and grid interconnection standards; and(3) Preserving necessary safeguards for public health, safety, environmental protection, and grid reliability.(d) Registration of power generation companies. –(1) Any person, corporation, or entity seeking to construct, own, or operate an electric generation facility in West Virginia, excluding public utilities subject to §24-2-11 of this code and energy facilities that produce power from an intermittent power source, shall register as a power generation company with the commission prior to commencing construction.(2) The commission shall provide a registration process which includes:(A) The legal name, business address, and principal place of business of the registrant;(B) A description of the proposed facility, including type, such as natural gas or coal, capacity in megawatts, location, and anticipated commercial operation date;(C) Contact information for primary and secondary emergency contacts and a regulatory representative;(D) Evidence of compliance with applicable federal requirements, including any Federal Energy Regulatory Commission registrations, if applicable; and(E) An affidavit attesting to the accuracy of the information provided.(3) The commission shall review the registration for completeness within 15 business days of receipt. If incomplete, the commission shall notify the registrant of deficiencies, and the registrant shall have 20 business days to cure. If not cured, the registration may be rejected without prejudice.(4) Upon approval, the commission shall issue a registration number, which is required for obtaining local permits, environmental approvals, and grid interconnection.(5) Registrants shall update their registration biennially or upon material changes, such as changes in ownership, facility modifications exceeding 10 percent of capacity, or cessation of operations.(6) The commission may establish reasonable fees for registration to cover administrative costs, not to exceed $1,000 per application.(7) Failure to register prior to construction may result in civil penalties not exceeding $5,000 per day, enforceable by the commission.(8) As used in this chapter, "intermittent power source" means an energy source whose electrical output is inherently variable, is not continuously available due to its dependence on external natural conditions, and cannot be fully controlled or dispatched on demand.(e) Environmental and Local Permitting. –(1) This section does not exempt electric generation facilities from complying with:(A) Air, water, and waste permits issued by the Department of Environmental Protection pursuant to chapters §22-1-1 et seq., §22A-1-1 et seq., and §22B-1-1 et seq. of this code;(B) Local zoning, building, and land-use permits from county commissions or municipal authority; and(C) Any applicable federal environmental or safety regulation.(2) The Department of Environmental Protection shall:(A) Prioritize and expedite permit reviews for dispatchable generation facilities, including without limitation natural gas and coal, and excluding energy facilities that produce power from an intermittent power source; and(B) Maintain a goal of issuing decisions within 180 days of complete application submission to facilitate timely development of the facilities, absent substantial public opposition or environmental concerns, as determined by the commission.(f) Grid interconnection. –(1) Electric generation facilities shall comply with interconnection standards and processes established by the applicable regional transmission organization, such as PJM Interconnection, for access to the interstate grid.(2) The commission shall coordinate with the regional transmission organization to ensure that interconnection studies and agreements do not unduly delay facility development and may intervene in cases of unreasonable delays.(g) Incentives for dispatchable generation. –(1) To encourage investment in reliable, dispatchable generation, the Division of Economic Development shall establish a program providing low-interest loans or grants for qualifying new natural gas or coal-fired facilities that add at least 100 megawatts of capacity.(2) Eligible projects must demonstrate a substantial positive impact on the local economy and employment, with priority given to facilities utilizing West Virginia-sourced fuel.(3) The Division shall report annually by December 1 to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance on program utilization and outcomes. NOTE: The purpose of this bill is to create the West Virginia Energy Freedom Act related to power generation companies in the state. (a) Short title. – This section is and may be cited as the "West Virginia Energy Freedom Act". (b) Legislative findings. – The Legislature finds that streamlining the approval process for new electric generation facilities, particularly dispatchable resources such as natural gas, and for coal-fired plants, is essential to promoting economic development, enhancing energy reliability, attracting investment in the state’s abundant natural resources, and supporting job creation throughout the state.(c) Purpose. – The purpose of this section is to reform the regulatory framework for new electric generation facilities by:(1) Eliminating the mandatory certificate of public convenience and necessity or siting certificate for a non-utility project;(2) Shifting oversight to a simplified registration process, local permitting, environmental compliance, and grid interconnection standards; and(3) Preserving necessary safeguards for public health, safety, environmental protection, and grid reliability.(d) Registration of power generation companies. –(1) Any person, corporation, or entity seeking to construct, own, or operate an electric generation facility in West Virginia, excluding public utilities subject to §24-2-11 of this code and energy facilities that produce power from an intermittent power source, shall register as a power generation company with the commission prior to commencing construction.(2) The commission shall provide a registration process which includes:(A) The legal name, business address, and principal place of business of the registrant;(B) A description of the proposed facility, including type, such as natural gas or coal, capacity in megawatts, location, and anticipated commercial operation date;(C) Contact information for primary and secondary emergency contacts and a regulatory representative;(D) Evidence of compliance with applicable federal requirements, including any Federal Energy Regulatory Commission registrations, if applicable; and(E) An affidavit attesting to the accuracy of the information provided.(3) The commission shall review the registration for completeness within 15 business days of receipt. If incomplete, the commission shall notify the registrant of deficiencies, and the registrant shall have 20 business days to cure. If not cured, the registration may be rejected without prejudice.(4) Upon approval, the commission shall issue a registration number, which is required for obtaining local permits, environmental approvals, and grid interconnection.(5) Registrants shall update their registration biennially or upon material changes, such as changes in ownership, facility modifications exceeding 10 percent of capacity, or cessation of operations.(6) The commission may establish reasonable fees for registration to cover administrative costs, not to exceed $1,000 per application.(7) Failure to register prior to construction may result in civil penalties not exceeding $5,000 per day, enforceable by the commission.(8) As used in this chapter, "intermittent power source" means an energy source whose electrical output is inherently variable, is not continuously available due to its dependence on external natural conditions, and cannot be fully controlled or dispatched on demand.(e) Environmental and Local Permitting. –(1) This section does not exempt electric generation facilities from complying with:(A) Air, water, and waste permits issued by the Department of Environmental Protection pursuant to chapters §22-1-1 et seq., §22A-1-1 et seq., and §22B-1-1 et seq. of this code;(B) Local zoning, building, and land-use permits from county commissions or municipal authority; and(C) Any applicable federal environmental or safety regulation.(2) The Department of Environmental Protection shall:(A) Prioritize and expedite permit reviews for dispatchable generation facilities, including without limitation natural gas and coal, and excluding energy facilities that produce power from an intermittent power source; and(B) Maintain a goal of issuing decisions within 180 days of complete application submission to facilitate timely development of the facilities, absent substantial public opposition or environmental concerns, as determined by the commission.(f) Grid interconnection. –(1) Electric generation facilities shall comply with interconnection standards and processes established by the applicable regional transmission organization, such as PJM Interconnection, for access to the interstate grid.(2) The commission shall coordinate with the regional transmission organization to ensure that interconnection studies and agreements do not unduly delay facility development and may intervene in cases of unreasonable delays.(g) Incentives for dispatchable generation. –(1) To encourage investment in reliable, dispatchable generation, the Division of Economic Development shall establish a program providing low-interest loans or grants for qualifying new natural gas or coal-fired facilities that add at least 100 megawatts of capacity.(2) Eligible projects must demonstrate a substantial positive impact on the local economy and employment, with priority given to facilities utilizing West Virginia-sourced fuel.(3) The Division shall report annually by December 1 to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance on program utilization and outcomes. NOTE: The purpose of this bill is to create the West Virginia Energy Freedom Act related to power generation companies in the state.

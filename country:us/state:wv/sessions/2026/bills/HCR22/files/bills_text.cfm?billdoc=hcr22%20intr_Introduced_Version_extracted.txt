Ward, Criss, Hanshaw (Mr. Speaker), Rohrbach, Fehrenbacher, Anderson, Zatezalo, and Riley)[Introduced February 19, 2026; referred to the Committee on Rules] Requesting the Joint Committee on Government and Finance to study policies to accelerate the ordering and deployment of electric generation equipment necessary to ensure grid reliability, support responsible economic growth, and advance West Virginia’s long term energy production goals. Whereas, West Virginia has long been a leading energy producing state and a net exporter of electricity; and Whereas, The State has articulated a long term objective of positioning West Virginia to support up to 50 gigawatts of electric generation capacity by the year 2050 as part of a comprehensive 50 50 energy growth strategy; andWhereas, Expanding electric generation capacity within the borders of West Virginia strengthens the state economy by retaining jobs, increasing tax revenues, and supporting upstream industries including natural gas production, coal mining, and related supply chains; and Whereas, Utilizing West Virginia produced natural gas for in state electric generation increases value added economic activity and keeps employment, severance taxes, property taxes, and payroll taxes within the state; and Whereas, There is renewed national discussion regarding advanced coal technologies and potential next generation coal fired facilities, and West Virginia maintains a strong interest in preserving optionality for coal based generation where economically feasible and technologically practical; andWhereas, National supply chain conditions have resulted in extended lead times of three to six years for large scale electric generation equipment, including heavy frame gas turbines and associated components; andWhereas, These extended manufacturing timelines require utilities and independent power producers to make substantial advance deposits and long term financial commitments prior to equipment delivery; andWhereas, Early ordering of generation equipment may be necessary to maintain reliability margins, support industrial expansion, and meet long term energy production goals; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia: That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance is hereby requested to study policy options that may accelerate the ordering and deployment of electric generation equipment within the state while maintaining fiscal discipline and protecting ratepayers; and, be it Further Resolved, That the study shall include, but not be limited to, the following policy mechanisms: (1) Limited State Backed Deposit Guarantees Examination of whether a narrowly tailored and capped state backed guarantee program could reduce financial risk associated with advance deposits for generation equipment, including assignment rights, clawback provisions, repayment requirements, and strict limits to protect taxpayers;(2) Regulatory Certainty with Ratepayer Protection Evaluation of whether existing regulatory frameworks provide sufficient clarity regarding recovery of prudently incurred generation costs, and whether any statutory adjustments are warranted to ensure timely infrastructure development while maintaining strong consumer safeguards and protection against unreasonable rate impacts;(3) Accelerated State Level Depreciation Analysis of whether limited duration accelerated depreciation schedules for electric generation assets ordered before a specified date could incentivize earlier capital commitment without direct state ownership of assets; (4) Applicability to Multiple Generation Technologies Review of how any proposed policies would apply across generation types, including natural gas fired generation, potential advanced coal technologies, and other dispatchable resources that support grid reliability;(5) Fiscal Guardrails and Risk Mitigation Assessment of appropriate caps, sunset provisions, performance benchmarks, and financial protections to ensure that any adopted policy supports infrastructure growth without exposing the State Treasury or regulated utility customers to undue risk;(6) Reliability and Economic Impact Evaluation of the projected impact of earlier equipment ordering on system reliability, industrial recruitment, job creation, retention of energy related employment, and long term rate stability; and be it Further Resolved, That the Joint Committee shall solicit input from the Public Service Commission, the Department of Commerce, regulated utilities, independent power producers, coal and natural gas industry representatives, consumer advocates, and other relevant stakeholders; and, be it Further Resolved, That the Committee shall report its findings, including any recommended legislation, to the Legislature during the next Regular Session.

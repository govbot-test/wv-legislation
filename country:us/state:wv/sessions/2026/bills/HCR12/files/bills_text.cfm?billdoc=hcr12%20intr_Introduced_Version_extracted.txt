Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 12(By Delegates Funkhouser, Ridenour, Horst, Roop, Hillenbrand, J. Cannon, Holstein, and Linville)[Introduced January 20, 2026, Referred to the committee on the Judiciary then Rules] Demanding Congress call a Convention of States to propose amendments to the Constitution of the United States to establish the United States Supreme Court as nine Justices. Whereas, Article V of the Constitution of the United States equally enables the States and Congress to propose Amendments to the Constitution of the United States; andWhereas, Article V of the Constitution of the United States mandates that Congress "on the Application of the Legislatures of two thirds of the several States, shall call a Convention for proposing Amendments, which, in either Case, shall be valid to all Intents and Purposes, as Part of this Constitution"; and Whereas, Article III of the Constitution of the United States does not set out the number of Supreme Court Justices; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Legislature calls on the Congress of the United States to do their duty under the Constitution and call a convention of States for proposing amendments to the Constitution of the United States, specifically amending the Constitution of the United States to provide that there shall be nine United States Supreme Court justices; and, be it Further Resolved, That the Clerk of the House forward copies of this resolution to the President and Secretary of the United States Senate, the Speaker and Clerk of the United States House of Representatives, and the members of the United States Senate and the United States House of Representatives of the State of West Virginia, and to the presiding officers of each of the legislative houses in the several States, requesting their cooperation in this endeavor.

Ward, Watt, White, Williams, Willis, Worrell, Young, and Zatezalo)[Introduced February 5, 2026] Honoring the life and public service of the Honorable Douglas John Skaff Jr. and expressing the condolences of the West Virginia House of Delegates on his passing.WHEREAS, Doug Skaff, Jr. was a proud native of South Charleston, West Virginia, whose deep roots in the Kanawha Valley informed a lifetime of commitment to the betterment of his community and the State of West Virginia; andWHEREAS, Doug was a graduate of West Virginia University and served as President of the Student Body, was the founder of the Mountaineer Maniacs, and co-founder of the WVUp All Night program; andWHEREAS, He earned a bachelor's degree in marketing in 2000, a master’s degree in industrial labor relations and business management in 2002, and an honorary degree in public service in 2012; andWHEREAS, He first answered the call to public service in 2008, being elected to the House of Delegates to represent the 30th District, and later the 35th and 57th Districts, serving multiple terms characterized by energetic advocacy for economic development, energy initiatives, and the well-being of West Virginia families; and WHEREAS, Doug rose to the position of Minority Leader of the House of Delegates, a role in which he demonstrated remarkable leadership, a keen legislative mind, and a unique ability to foster dialogue across the aisle in pursuit of the common good; andWHEREAS, Beyond his legislative career, Doug was a dynamic businessman and entrepreneur, contributing to the state’s economic landscape through his work in the building materials industry, Skaff family enterprises, and as a media executive, bringing the same passion to the private sector that he brought to the House Floor; andWHEREAS, Doug was known for his infectious energy, his unwavering optimism, and his deep devotion to his family, being a loving husband to his wife, Marisa, a dedicated father to his three sons – Andrew, Elias, and Alexander; as a son to his parents, Doug, Sr, and Maryann, and brother to Teresa and Thomas; andWHEREAS, His legacy will be remembered not only through the laws he helped craft but through the friendships he forged and the mentorship he provided to many who sought to serve the Mountain State; therefore, be itResolved by the House of Delegates:That the House of Delegates hereby honors the life, service, and legacy of the Honorable Doug J. Skaff, Jr. and expresses its most sincere condolences to his family and friends; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Clerk of the House of Delegates prepare a certified copy of this resolution to be presented to the family of Doug Skaff, Jr. as a token of the high esteem in which his memory is held by the members of this body.

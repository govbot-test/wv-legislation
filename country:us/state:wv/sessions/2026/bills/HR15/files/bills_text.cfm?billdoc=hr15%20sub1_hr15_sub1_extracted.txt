Red = new code to be enacted Committee SubstituteforHOUSE RESOLUTION 15(By Delegates G. Ward, Statler, and Stephens)[Originating in the Committee on Energy and Public Works; Reported on February 24, 2026] Expressing support for the I-68 Energy Manufacturing CorridorWhereas, The West Virginia Route 2 (WV Route 2) / Interstate 68 (I-68) Authority was established by the WV Legislature on April 12, 1997, with the passage of House Bill 2539 and signed by Governor Cecil Underwood on May 2, 1997; being vested with the power to promote the Corridors development; andWhereas, The extension of I-68 from its junction with I-79 near Morgantown, West Virginia, to WV Route 2 within Marshall County, West Virginia, to span the Ohio River where most practical, has been sought after even before the creation of the authority; andWhereas, The Shale Crescent region of Ohio, West Virginia, and Pennsylvania is among the largest natural gas producing regions of the country, and is a major producer of natural gas liquids; andWhereas, The Corridor has the potential to accelerate revenues of the public and private sector alike, creating thousands of jobs in the Shale Crescent region, thereby linking the center of Appalachian energy production to the Midwest for streamlined interstate commerce; andWhereas, The White House arranged meetings with the United States Department of Transportation, Department of Commerce, the Department of Energy in Washington, D.C., and the WV 2/I-68 Authority, and obtained their support to expedite The Corridor under President Trump's first term; andWhereas, The Corridor would advance The Executive’s current agenda to develop infrastructure necessary for economic growth and opportunities for re-shoring energy, manufacturing, and the development of AI technologies; andWhereas, The Corridor is crucial as natural gas extraction from the Shale Crescent region is expected to continue to rise, placing additional unanticipated stress on highway infrastructure and many secondary roads; therefore, be itResolved by the House of Delegates:That the House of Delegates hereby expresses support for the I-68 Energy Manufacturing Corridor; and be itFurther Resolved, That the House of Delegates fully supports and urges the White House, Governor Patrick Morrisey, the Legislature, and representatives of both state and federal government bodies to do everything in their power to secure funding to actualize the I-68 Energy Manufacturing Corridor from the junction with I-79 near Morgantown, West Virginia, to Marshall County, West Virginia and across the Ohio River where most practical, meeting I-77 near Caldwell, Ohio, and eventually westward to I-75 between Dayton and Middletown, Ohio.

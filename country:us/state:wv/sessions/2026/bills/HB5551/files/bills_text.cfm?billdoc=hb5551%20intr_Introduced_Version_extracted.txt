Ward, Horst, Phillips, and White[Introduced February 16, 2026; referred to the Committee on the Judiciary]A BILL to amend the Code of West Virginia, 1931, as amended, by adding a new article, designated §17B-2C-1, relating to identification cards; providing a short title; providing a purpose; establishing directives to protect licenses and identification cards; authorizing rule making; providing severability; and providing an effective date.Be it enacted by the Legislature of West Virginia: ARTICLE 2C. PROTECTING State Licensing Act. §17B-2C-1. Protection of state issued non-REAL ID licenses and identification cards. (a) Short title. – This act shall be known and cited as the "Protecting State Licensing Act". (b) Purpose. –  The purpose of this act is to safeguard state sovereignty and the citizens of the state by protecting non-REAL ID compliant licenses and identification cards. The act prohibits the storage and sharing of sensitive personal and identifiable data as well as the capture of biometric photographs. The Act further protects against federal changes to the REAL ID program.(c) Protection of Non-REAL ID driver licenses and photo identification licenses. – (1) The department shall continue to offer and make available a driver license or photo identification license that is not compliant with the federal REAL IQ Act of 2005  PL 109-13. and any subsequent amendments thereto. Such licenses shall be clearly marked as "NOT FOR REAL ID ACT PURPOSES' and shall be valid for all purposes under West Virginia law, including operation of a motor vehicle and proof of identity.(2) The department and its agents are prohibited from collecting, capturing, storing, or using any biometric identifiers in connection with the issuance of a non-REAL ID compliant drivers license or photo identification license. This includes, but is not limited to, biometric facial image data consistent with the REAL ID Act's implementing regulation requiring compliance with ISO/IEC information data interchange formats, face image data or any successor standard. biometric identifiers, facial recognition data, iris scans, fingerprints, voiceprints, or any measurable biological or behavioral characteristic used for recognition of an individual.(3) The department shall not retain copies of original or secondary source documents used to verify identity when issuing a non-REAL ID-compliant driver license or photo identification license. Such documents include, but are not limited to, birth certificates, social security cards, utility bills, financial statements, tax records, or other documents that may be used to prove residency, legal presence, or identity.(4) Except for any valid purposes related to the National Law Enforcement Telecommunications System, any information collected from an individual who obtains a non-REAL IQ-compliant driver license or photo identification license shall not be accessible to, shared with, or stored in any interstate, federal, or international database system including out net limited to the State Pointer Exchange Service.(5) Nothing in this act shall be construed to prohibit the department from issuing REAL ID-compliant driver licenses or photo identification licenses to individuals who voluntarily request them and meet the applicable federal requirements, nor to restrict an eligible individual from applying for a non-REAL ID-compliant license if they previously obtained a REAL ID-compliant license.(6) The division shall ensure that all individuals applying for a driver's license or photo  identification license are informed verbally and in writing of their right to choose between a REAL ID-compliant license and a non-REAL ID-compliant license. Any sign, display, brochure, electronic interface, or public communication that describes or promotes the REAL ID program must clearly state that participation in the REAL ID program is voluntary and that non-REAL ID-compliant driver licenses and photo identification licenses are available as an alternative. All applicants must be given a copy of the complete list of current TSA acceptable IDs.(7) The division shall propose rules for legislative approval in accordance with the provisions of §29A-3-1 et seq. of this code to implement the provisions of this article and shall provide annual training to department staff regarding its provisions. A willful violation of this article by any agent or employee of the division shall be subject to disciplinary action under departmental policy, including potential termination for repeated or willful noncompliance.(8) The division is prohibited from entering into any agreement or memorandum of understanding that would conflict with the rights and protections set forth in this act.(9) No state entity may require the use of a digital driver’s license or digital ID for any purpose.  A physical driver’s license or identification card must be accepted for all identification purposes in this state.(d) Severability. – If any provision of this act or the application thereof to any  person or circumstance is held invalid, such invalidity shall not affect other provisions or application of the act which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application. and to that end the provisions of this act are declared to be severable.(e) Prohibition on future changes. –  If the federal REAL ID Act. Public Law 109-13 Division B is repealed, or if the definition of "official purpose" in Code of Federal Regulations  title 6 part 37 is changed, or if federal law or rules are changed to prohibit  state issuance of non-compliant licenses or identification then the division and any other relevant state agencies must immediately rescind all compliance measures with the REAL ID program and cease from issuing REAL ID compliant licenses.(f) Effective date. – This act takes effect July 1, 2026. The division shall comply with subsection (c)(2) no later than October 1, 2026. NOTE: The purpose of this bill is to safeguard state sovereignty and the citizens of the state by protecting non-REAL ID compliant licenses and identification cards.Strike-throughs indicate language that would be stricken from a heading or the present law and underscoring indicates new language that would be added.

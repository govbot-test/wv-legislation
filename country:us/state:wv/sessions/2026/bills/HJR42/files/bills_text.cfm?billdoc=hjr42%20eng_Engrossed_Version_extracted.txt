Red = new code to be enacted WEST virginia legislature2026 regular sessionENGROSSEDforHouse Joint Resolution 42By Delegates Criss, Riley, Gearheart, Anderson, Canterbury, Cooper, Dittman, Fehrenbacher, Hall, Hornbuckle, and Rohrbach[Originating in the Committee on Finance;February 27, 2026] Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of the State of West Virginia amending article X thereof, by adding thereto a new section, designated section one-d, relating to homestead exemption increase; numbering and designating such proposed amendment; and providing a summarized statement of the purpose of such proposed amendment.Resolved by the Legislature of West Virginia, two thirds of the members elected to each house agreeing thereto:That the question of ratification or rejection of an amendment to the Constitution of the State of West Virginia be submitted to the voters of the state at the next general election to be held in the year 2026, which proposed amendment is that article X thereof be amended by adding thereto a new section, designated section one-d, to read as follows: ARTICLE X. TAXATION AND FINANCE. §1d. Homestead exemption amendment of 2026. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this Constitution to the contrary, the first $50,000 of assessed valuation of any real property, or of personal property in the form of a mobile home, used exclusively for residential purposes and occupied by the owner or one of the owners thereof as his or her residence who is a citizen of this state and who is 65 years of age or older or is permanently and totally disabled as that term may be defined by the Legislature, shall be exempt from ad valorem property taxation, subject to the requirements, limitations, and conditions as shall be prescribed by general law.Notwithstanding any other provision of this Constitution to the contrary, the Legislature may provide by general law for an exemption from ad valorem property taxation in an amount not to exceed the first $50,000 of value of any real property, or of personal property in the form of a mobile home, used exclusively for residential purposes and occupied by the owner or one of the owners thereof as his or her residence who is a citizen of this state, and who is under 65 years of age and not totally and permanently disabled.  Upon enactment of the general law, this exemption shall only apply to the property in any county in which the property was appraised at its value asof the date determined by the Legislature, and this exemption shall be phased in over the period of time not to exceed five years from the date the property was so appraised, or such longer time as the Legislature may determine by general law.  In no event is any one person and his or her spouse, or one homestead entitled to more than one exemption under these provisions.  In addition, these provisions are subject to the requirements, limitations, and conditions as shall be prescribed by general law.The Legislature has the authority to provide by general law for property tax relief to citizens of this state who are tenants of residential or farm property.Resolved further, That in accordance with the provisions of article eleven, chapter three of the Code of West Virginia, 1931, as amended, such amendment is hereby numbered "Amendment No. 1" and designated as the "Homestead Exemption Increase Amendment" and the purpose of the proposed amendment is summarized as follows:  "The purpose of this amendment is to change the language relating to the amount of the Homestead Exemption from "the first $20,000" to " the first $50,000" of property eligible for the Homestead Exemption."

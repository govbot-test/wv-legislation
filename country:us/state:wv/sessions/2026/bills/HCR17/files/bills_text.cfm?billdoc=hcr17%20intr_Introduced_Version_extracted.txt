Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 17(By Delegates Pritt, Clay, B. Smith, Parsons, Eldridge, Adkins, Hornbuckle, Fehrenbacher, Flanigan, Martin, and Vance)[Introduced February 10, 2026; referredto the Committee on Rules]Requesting the Joint Committee on Government and Finance study the safety measures for pedestrians on the New River Gorge Bridge.Whereas, A study should be undertaken to determine the feasibility, anticipated effectiveness, and potential costs involved to install and maintain pedestrian safety measures to the New River Gorge Bridge; andWhereas, In conducting the study, the committee shall consult with the Bridge Day Commission to consider whether the subject measures can be installed and maintained in a manner that will permit Bridge Day activities to continue and, if so, the extent to which the potential costs of the subject measures would differ if they were installed in a manner that would not permit Bridge Day activities to continue; andWhereas, The study should determine the availability and extent of federal funding to incorporate the subject measures; andWhereas, The study should look at the feasibility of allocating funds from the State Road Fund; seek or obtain grants to facilitate the study; enter into agreements with other governmental agencies, committees, and educational institutions for the collection and analysis of information; and contract with private persons or companies; therefore, be Resolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Joint Committee on Government and Finance is hereby requested to study the safety measures for persons on the New River Gorge Bridge; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Joint Committee on government and Finance report to the regular session of the Legislature, 2027, on its findings, conclusions, and recommendations, together with drafts of any legislation necessary to effectuate its recommendations; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the expenses necessary to conduct this study, to prepare a report, and to draft necessary legislation be paid from legislative appropriations to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance.

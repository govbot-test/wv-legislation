Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 27(By Delegates Jeffries, T. Howell, Riley, Jennings, Green, Campbell, Stephens, and Moore)[Introduced March 3, 2026; referred to the Committee on Rules] Requesting a comprehensive legislative study of disaster recovery and flood resiliency funding, coordination, statutory compliance, and disaster case management capacity within the State of West Virginia.Whereas, The State of West Virginia has established the State Resiliency Office and the State Resiliency Officer pursuant to §29-31-1 et seq. of the Code of West Virginia to coordinate disaster recovery and resiliency missions, administer the West Virginia Disaster Recovery Trust Fund and the West Virginia Flood Resiliency Trust Fund, and serve as the Governor’s primary representative on disaster recovery and resiliency matters; andWhereas, The statute grants the State Resiliency Officer broad authority to receive, administer, prioritize, and disburse funds for disaster recovery, flood mitigation, hazard mitigation, and resiliency planning, and to lead long-term recovery efforts following state and federal disaster declarations; andWhereas, The Legislature has a responsibility to ensure that all funds available for flood resiliency, hazard mitigation, and disaster recovery are fully identified, aggressively pursued, efficiently administered, and deposited and expended in accordance with statutory intent; andWhereas, Changes at the federal level, including administrative and structural changes within the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA), may significantly affect the availability, structure, and requirements of disaster recovery and hazard mitigation funding impacting West Virginia; andWhereas, The Legislature seeks to ensure that West Virginia is positioned to maximize funding drawdown, strengthen internal recovery capacity, reduce reliance on external entities when appropriate, and accelerate recovery outcomes for its citizens; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Legislature hereby requests a comprehensive study to evaluate the performance, coordination, funding strategy, and statutory compliance of the State Resiliency Office and related state agencies in carrying out disaster recovery and flood resiliency responsibilities; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study shall include, but not be limited to:1. A comprehensive inventory of all federal, state, and other funding sources available to states for flood resiliency, hazard mitigation, disaster recovery, and long-term community resilience, including competitive, formula, and discretionary programs;2. An analysis of the specific funds West Virginia has applied for, been awarded, drawn down, declined, or not pursued over the past ten years, including the reasons for non-participation or non-deposit into the Disaster Recovery Trust Fund or Flood Resiliency Trust Fund;3. A review of whether funds eligible for administration by the State Resiliency Office are being deposited, administered, and disbursed consistent with statutory requirements under §29-31-1 et seq.;4. An evaluation of whether the State Resiliency Officer is fully exercising statutory authority to lead, coordinate, and direct disaster recovery and resiliency efforts across all relevant state agencies;5. An assessment of interagency coordination and whether the State Resiliency Office is effectively utilizing its statutory authority to draw upon other departments, divisions, and agencies for research, planning, and implementation support;6. A forward-looking analysis of anticipated federal policy and structural changes within FEMA and related agencies, and the projected impact of such changes on West Virginia’s disaster recovery and mitigation funding, planning requirements, and administrative responsibilities; and7. Recommendations for statutory, administrative, or structural reforms necessary to ensure that the State Resiliency Officer serves as the clear lead entity for statewide disaster recovery and resiliency efforts as contemplated in code; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Legislature respectfully requests a comprehensive feasibility study to determine whether the State of West Virginia should require that all personnel within the West Virginia Emergency Management Division (WVEMD) and the Community Outreach and Development Division within the West Virginia Department of Commerce receive cross-training as disaster case managers to support response and long-term recovery efforts during declared states of emergency; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study shall evaluate:1. The projected cost of implementing mandatory disaster case management training, including initial certification, ongoing continuing education, and administrative costs;2. The potential cost savings to the state resulting from reduced reliance on nonprofit organizations, contractors, and third-party recovery entities;3. The operational feasibility of assigning disaster case management responsibilities to existing personnel during emergency declarations;4. The anticipated impact on speed, coordination, and overall effectiveness of disaster recovery efforts, including whether such cross-training would allow West Virginia to recover more efficiently and in a timelier manner; and5. Recommended staffing models, training standards, funding mechanisms, and performance measures necessary to successfully implement such a structure; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study shall include comparative analysis of other states’ disaster case management models, an estimated return on investment, and recommendations to the Legislature regarding whether such a model would strengthen West Virginia’s disaster resilience and recovery capacity; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Legislature may utilize its existing flood resiliency and disaster recovery collaborative partners, subject matter experts, and intergovernmental entities to conduct or assist in conducting this study, and that findings and recommendations shall be reported to the appropriate legislative committees with proposed statutory or budgetary recommendations.

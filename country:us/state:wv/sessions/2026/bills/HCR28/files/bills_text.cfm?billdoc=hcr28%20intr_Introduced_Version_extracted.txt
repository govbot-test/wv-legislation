Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 28(By Delegates Dillon, Coop-Gonzalez, Butler, Dean, Anders, Martin, Clay, Masters, Jordan, Pritt, G. Howell, Bridges, Fehrenbacher, Miller, and Vance)[Introduced March 5, 2026; referred to the Committee on Rules] Requesting the Legislature to direct the Office of the Attorney General and Office of the Treasurer to collaboratively produce a study of the state appropriations and budget of the current year and previous five year period for the purpose of identifying each budget line or supplementary appropriation item.Whereas, The Constitution of West Virginia mandates certain core functions of government, certain individual rights, and spending associated with protecting the same; and Whereas, The Constitution of West Virginia permits additional spending appropriations and authorizations beyond those arising from constitutional mandates and core functions of government; andWhereas, Taxpayer dollars should always be collected and budgeted with the first priority given to fulfilling constitutional requirements before additional appropriations can be authorized; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the Legislature directs the Office of the Attorney General and Office of the Treasurer to collaboratively produce a study of the state appropriations and budget of the current year and previous five year period for the purpose of identifying each budget line or supplementary appropriation item; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the study shall include an examination of the following:(1) Direct and explicit constitutionally mandated spending;(2) Spending arising from fulfilling core functions of government as expressed in the Constitution of West Virginia;(3) Spending permitted in pursuit of a policy goal, but not arising from fulfillment of constitutional obligation or core constitutional state governmental functions; and(4) Spending with historical precedent, but consisting of a more speculative or nebulous purpose; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Office of the Attorney General and the Office of the State Treasurer shall report its findings, conclusions, and recommendations to the Joint Committee on Government and Finance no later than December 21, 2026.

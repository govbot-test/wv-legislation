Red = new code to be enacted WEST virginia legislature2026 regular sessionIntroducedSenate Joint Resolution 19By Senators Phillips and Deeds[Introduced February 5, 2026; referred to the Committee on the Judiciary; and then to the Committee on Finance]Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of the State of West Virginia, amending section one, article VII thereof, relating to election of the executive department, providing that no person may be elected as, or serve as, Governor, unless that person was born in West Virginia; numbering and designating such proposed amendment; and providing a summarized statement of the purpose of such proposed amendment.Resolved by the Legislature of West Virginia, two thirds of the members elected to each house agreeing thereto:That the question of ratification or rejection of an amendment to the Constitution of the State of West Virginia be submitted to the voters of the state at the next general election to be held in the year 2026, which proposed is that section one, article VII thereof, be amended to read as follows.ARTICLE VII. 7-1. Executive department. The executive department shall consist of a governor, secretary of state, auditor, treasurer, commissioner of agriculture and attorney general, who shall be ex officio reporter of the court of appeals. No person may be elected as or serve as governor, unless that person was born in West Virginia. Their terms of office shall be four years, and shall commence on the first Monday after the second Wednesday of January next after their election. They shall reside at the seat of government during their terms of office, keep there the public records, books and papers pertaining to their respective offices, and shall perform such duties as may be prescribed by law. Resolved further, That in accordance with the provisions of article eleven, chapter three of the Code of West Virginia, 1931, as amended, such amendment is hereby numbered "Amendment 1" and designated as the "Governor born in West Virginia Amendment" and the purpose of the proposed amendment is summarized as follows:  "Candidates for governor shall be born in West Virginia to eligible for that office." NOTE: The purpose of this resolution is to provide that candidates for governor shall be born in West Virginia in order to be eligible for office of Governor.Strike-throughs indicate language that would be stricken from a heading or the present law and underscoring indicates new language that would be added.

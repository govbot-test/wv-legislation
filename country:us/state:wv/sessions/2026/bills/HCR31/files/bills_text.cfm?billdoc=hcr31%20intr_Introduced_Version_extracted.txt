Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 31By Delegates Hillenbrand, D. Smith, Watt, Ridenour, and Adkins[Introduced March 10, 2026; referred to the Committee on Rules] Urging the United States Congress to recognize United States Armed Forces members who served in Honduras from 1981 to 1992 by authorizing the issuance of the Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal.Whereas, The State of West Virginia has a long and proud history of honoring its veterans and believes that all servicemembers who serve in hostile environments should receive the recognition they have earned through their sacrifices; andWhereas, The United States military maintained a continuous and significant presence in Honduras throughout the 1980s and early 1990s, providing training and conducting joint military exercises to strengthen regional security; andWhereas, This presence served as a critical deterrent to regional aggression and the spread of the Sandinista regime's influence, while simultaneously supporting democratic values through civil action and community support in Honduras; andWhereas, Declassified reports confirm that during this period, the region was characterized by high tension, including maritime clashes and gunfire exchanges between neighboring states in the Gulf of Fonseca; andWhereas, United States troops in Honduras were equipped with live ammunition and provided direct combat support and training to the Honduran military in a high-risk environment; andWhereas, These servicemembers frequently experienced hostile fire and were placed in positions of imminent danger due to the volatility of the region; andWhereas, The reality of these hostilities is evidenced by the fact that at least one United States servicemember was awarded the Purple Heart for injuries sustained from terrorist activity in Honduras; andWhereas, Records indicate that at least 46 American servicemembers lost their lives while serving in Honduras between January 1, 1981, and February 1, 1992, due to terrorist activities and other hazardous circumstances inherent to the mission; andWhereas, Despite the clear presence of hostility and the loss of life, these veterans have not yet been authorized to receive the Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal for their service in Honduras during this period; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the West Virginia Legislature hereby urges the United States Congress to support efforts to authorize the issuance of the Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal, or a similar recognition of service in an area of hostility, to all United States troops who served in Honduras from 1981 to 1992; and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Clerk of the House of Representatives is hereby directed to forward a copy of this resolution to the President of the United States Senate, the Speaker of the United States House of Representatives, and to each member of the West Virginia Congressional Delegation.

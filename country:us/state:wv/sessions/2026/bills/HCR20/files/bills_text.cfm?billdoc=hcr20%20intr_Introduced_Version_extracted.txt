Red = new code to be enacted HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION 20(By Delegate Green)[Introduced February 17, 2026; Referredto the Committee on Energy and Public Words then Rules] Designating the City of Gary water and wastewater systems for emergency intervention under the State Utility Accountability and Improvement Program.Whereas, The Legislature has enacted comprehensive utility assistance legislation creating the State Utility Accountability and Improvement Program under Article 24-2I of the Code of West Virginia; andWhereas, The City of Gary water and wastewater systems serve residents of Gary, McDowell County, a community that has faced significant economic challenges; andWhereas, Gary systems face serious challenges including aging infrastructure, limited rate base, and difficulty maintaining adequate operational and financial capacity; andWhereas, Residents have reported water quality concerns, including discolored water, indicating the need for immediate infrastructure improvements and operational changes; andWhereas, The systems have experienced compliance challenges with health and safety standards; andWhereas, Financial management of the systems has raised concerns regarding the appropriate separation of utility and general municipal funds; andWhereas, There is an active Public Service Commission proceeding, Case No. 25-0611-W-DU, examining the status of the Gary systems; andWhereas, Prompt state assistance is necessary to protect public health and ensure Gary residents receive safe, adequate water and wastewater service; andWhereas, The Gary systems will serve as an initial demonstration of the state intervention mechanisms created by the State Utility Accountability and Improvement Program, fulfilling the pilot phase described in §24-2I-9a of the Code of West Virginia; therefore, be itResolved by the Legislature of West Virginia:That the City of Gary water system and the City of Gary wastewater system are hereby designated for emergency intervention under the State Utility Accountability and Improvement Program established by Article 24-2I of the Code of West Virginia; and, be itFurther Resolved, That upon adoption of this resolution and approval by the Governor:(1) The State Utility Management Corps shall conduct an emergency assessment of the Gary systems within seven days;(2) The Gary systems shall be classified as Tier 4 utilities under §24-2I-4, subject to Gary’s right to appeal the classification;(3) If classified as Tier 4, the team shall determine whether conservatorship is necessary and, if so, file a petition with the Public Service Commission as provided in §24-2I-6;(4) All provisions of Article 24-2I shall apply to the Gary systems, including expedited timelines, conservator powers, improvement plan requirements, Priority Utility Docket procedures, and funding priorities;(5) The Gary water and wastewater systems shall receive first priority for grants and loans from the Distressed Utilities Account;(6) The conservator, if appointed, shall file monthly reports with the Legislature as provided in §24-2I-6(k), including reports to the Speaker of the House of Delegates, President of the Senate, and the Joint Committee on Technology and Infrastructure;(7) The Water Development Authority is authorized to advance up to three million dollars from the Distressed Utilities Account for emergency repairs and improvements to the Gary systems, subject to available funds;(8) The City of Gary, including its mayor, city council, and all city employees, shall cooperate with the team, conservator, and Public Service Commission in all aspects of the assessment and conservatorship; and(9) The team shall collaborate from the outset with the most likely long-term operator of the systems, whether the existing governing body, a neighboring utility, or another qualified entity, to facilitate an orderly transition upon completion of conservatorship; and, be itFurther Resolved, That this designation does not preclude or delay Public Service Commission proceedings regarding ownership of the Gary systems in Case No. 25-0611-W-DU or any successor case. The commission may determine ownership questions while conservatorship is ongoing. If the commission orders acquisition, conservatorship shall continue until transfer occurs pursuant to the mutual agreement process in §24-2I-6(o); and, be itFurther Resolved, That the team shall prepare a preliminary evaluation report on the Gary intervention within six months and a comprehensive evaluation report after eighteen months, to be submitted to the Legislature, Governor, Public Service Commission, Joint Committee on Technology and Infrastructure, and the public. These reports shall fulfill the evaluation gate requirement of §24-2I-9a(c); and, be itFurther Resolved, That the Clerk of the House of Delegates forward certified copies of this resolution to the Governor, the Water Development Authority, the Public Service Commission, the Mayor and City Council of Gary, and McDowell County Public Service District.

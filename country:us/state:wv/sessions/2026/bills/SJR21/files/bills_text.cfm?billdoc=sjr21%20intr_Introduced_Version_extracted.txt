Red = new code to be enacted WEST virginia legislature2026 regular sessionIntroducedSenate Joint Resolution 21By Senator Willis[Introduced February 20, 2026; referredto the Committee on the Judiciary; and then to the Committee on Finance]Proposing an amendment to the Constitution of the State of West Virginia, amending article X thereof by adding thereto a new section, designated section 13, relating to the Taxpayer's Bill of Rights; providing state government may not increase expenditures more than the growth of the population plus inflation; providing for the mailing of information to registered voters; numbering and designating such proposed amendment; and providing a summarized statement of the purpose of such proposed amendment.Resolved by the Legislature of West Virginia, two thirds of the members elected to each house agreeing thereto:That the question of ratification or rejection of an amendment to the Constitution of the State of West Virginia be submitted to the voters of the state at the next general election to be held in the year 2026, which proposed amendment is that article X thereof be amended by adding thereto a new section, designated section 13, to be read as follows:ARTICLE X.§13. Taxpayers' Bill of Rights.An amendment to the West Virginia Constitution to require state government may not increase expenditures more than the growth of the population plus inflation.Resolved further, That in accordance with the provisions of article eleven, chapter three of the Code of West Virginia, 1931, as amended, such amendment is hereby numbered "Amendment 1" and designated as the "Taxpayers' Bill of Rights Amendment" and the purpose of the proposed amendment is summarized as follows:  "To require state government may not increase expenditures more than the growth of the population plus inflation." NOTE: The purpose of this resolution is to establish the Taxpayers' Bill of Rights, establishing a tax and expenditure limitation that provides that state government may not increase expenditures more than the growth of the population plus inflation.Strike-throughs indicate language that would be stricken from a heading or the present law and underscoring indicates new language that would be added.
